Materials

  • Bucket
  • Old towel
  • New filter
  • Soap and water

Filter specs

Link
  • 20 microns (mu m)
  • 4.5 inch diameter
  • 20 inch height

Instructions

(10 - 15 minutes)
  1. make sure you have all the materials
  2. place bucket and towel under the filter
  3. close inlet valve
  4. run water until it slows down (you don't have to wait until it fully stops)
  5. close outlet valve
  6. open bypass valve (you'll need water to clean out the shell)
  7. press red button to relieve water pressure
  8. take the big wrench thing that looks like a tennis racket and snug it up to the top of the shell
  9. untwist the shell (it is very heavy)
  10. place shell in bucket
  11. remove filter and drain excess water into bucket
  12. clean out shell with soap and water
  13. insert new filter into shell
  14. screw back into place hand tight (do NOT use wrench to tighten)
  15. close bypass valve first
  16. open inlet valve second
  17. open outlet valve last
  18. check for leaks
  19. dump old water
  20. update this page with learnings and log entry

When to change

There are a couple conditions for changing:
  • If you notice a dip in water pressure in the house
  • Check it roughly every 3 months. Change it if it looks dark brown / black
  • If 12 months go by since you last changed it
