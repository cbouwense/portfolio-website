Materials

  • Bucket
  • Old towel
  • New filter
  • Soap and water

Filter specs

  • 20 microns (mu m)
  • 4.5 inch diameter
  • 20 inch height
  • Got the last one at Home Depot aisle 33
  • Should be like $25 (Feb 2025 dollars)

Instructions

(5 - 15 minutes)
  1. make sure you have all the materials
  2. place bucket and towel under the filter
  3. close inlet valve
  4. run water until it slows down (you don't have to wait until it fully stops)
  5. close outlet valve
  6. press red button to relieve water pressure
  7. take the big wrench thing that looks like a tennis racket and snug it up to the top of the shell.
  8. untwist the shell. (it is very heavy)
  9. place shell in bucket.
  10. remove filter and drain excess water into bucket.
  11. clean out shell with soap and water.
  12. insert new filter into shell
  13. screw back into place with tennis racket wrench
  14. open inlet valve first
  15. open outlet valve
  16. check for leaks
  17. dump old water

When to change

There are a couple conditions for changing:
  • If you notice a dip in water pressure in the house
  • Check it roughly every 3 months. Change it if it looks dark brown / black
  • If 12 months go by since you last changed it
